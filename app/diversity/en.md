## TLDR;

In this open-source project, diversity is welcome. Regardless of your gender, sexual orientation, ethnic background, religion, age or any other personal characteristic, you are welcome to contribute to this project. We believe that diversity is a strength and that different perspectives and experiences enrich our community.

## We are an inclusive community

We are open to all contributors, regardless of their gender, sexual orientation, ethnic background, religion, age or any other personal characteristic.
If you wish to contribute to this project, know that you are welcome, that no discrimination will be tolerated towards you, and that you are here to do good work, not to be judged on your personal characteristics.

We are committed to creating an inclusive and respectful work environment for all contributors. We do not tolerate any form of discrimination, harassment or inappropriate behavior. We encourage diversity of ideas, opinions and experiences, and we believe this contributes to the quality of our work.

## Why this page?

While preparing [a conference on open-source](/open-source-howto/dist/) (in French), I came across an article ["Where are women in Open Source?" (openforumeurope.org)](https://openforumeurope.org/where-are-women-of-open-source/) which cites a 2006 study by [Bianca Trinkenreich](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2103.08763):

> only 1.5% of OSS contributors were women, compared to 28% in proprietary software.

In the explanations, they note that:

- Women always have more family responsibilities, and therefore less time to contribute to open-source,
- There are few role models of women doing open-source, and therefore few women follow them in this adventure,
- There is still a lot of discrimination and sexism in open-source communities.

And therefore only truly experienced women dare to take the step.

### A small step towards inclusivity

This project will not revolutionize the first point, but by clearly displaying my opinions on (non-)discrimination, I hope to encourage more people to contribute to open-source. The article talks particularly about women, but this is possibly the tip of the iceberg. I therefore broaden the message to all people who feel excluded from open-source, and hope to encourage more diversity in open-source in general.

Generally, when [infrastructure is more adapted, usage is facilitated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cycling_in_the_Netherlands). By clearly displaying that diversity is welcome in this project, I hope to create a more adapted infrastructure for people who feel excluded from open-source, and thus facilitate their contributions.
